5 Forest Bathing Techniques to Try on Your Next Hike

By the Namaste in Nature team · 4 min read

Forest bathing, or shinrin-yoku, isn't about exercise or reaching a summit — it's about being present with the forest through your senses. Here are five simple techniques you can try on your very next walk in the woods.

1. Leave the destination behind

Pick a short stretch of trail and give yourself permission to go slowly. There's no goal here but to notice. Let your pace match your breath.

2. Tune into sound

Stop walking. Close your eyes for thirty seconds and count the distinct sounds you can hear — wind, water, birdsong, your own breath. This single practice quiets a busy mind fast.

The forest doesn't ask anything of you. It simply invites you to arrive.

3. Touch the textures

Run your hand along bark, moss, and stone. Physical sensation pulls you out of your head and into the present moment.

4. Breathe the air in fully

Take a few slow, deep breaths through your nose. Forest air carries compounds called phytoncides that research links to lower stress and a calmer nervous system.

5. Pause and simply watch

Find a spot to sit. Watch light move through the leaves, or a stream slip over rocks. Five unhurried minutes can reset your whole day.
